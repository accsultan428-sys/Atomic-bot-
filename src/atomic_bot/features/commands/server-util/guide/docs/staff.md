## Staff Guidelines

---

### Handling Ask Staff Tickets

> The Ask Staff channel is the go-to place for members and buyers with general questions. When a ticket comes in, here's what you do.

**1.** When you receive an Ask Staff notification, press the **Answer** button to claim the ticket.
**2.** A dedicated thread will be created automatically. Begin assisting the member inside that thread.
**3.** Respond professionally and informatively. Use language the member can easily understand — avoid unnecessary jargon.
**4.** If the issue cannot be resolved at your level, escalate it to a PR Associate or the appropriate division — do not leave the member without a response.
**5.** Once the member's question is fully addressed and there are no remaining concerns, close the ticket.

---

### Handling Priority Support Tickets

> Priority Support is reserved for buyers experiencing issues with their scripts. These tickets require prompt and attentive handling.

**1.** When you receive an "Opened Priority Support" notification, press **Join Ticket**.
**2.** Select **Claim** to indicate that you are the one handling this ticket.
**3.** Identify the buyer's issue and assist them in resolving it. Stay patient and communicate clearly throughout.
**4.** If the issue is outside your knowledge or scope, consult internally or escalate to the appropriate staff — never leave the buyer without a response or update.
**5.** Once the issue is resolved and the buyer has no further concerns, close the ticket.

---

### Processing Purchase Tickets

> Purchase tickets are opened by members who want to buy a script. Handle each transaction carefully and systematically — accuracy here directly affects trust in Atomic.

**1.** When you receive an "Opened Purchase Ticket" notification, press **Join Ticket**, then select **Claim**.
**2.** Show the QRIS code using the `.q` command. Always verify the current price against the #price channel or latest announcements before proceeding.
**3.** Once the customer sends proof of payment, verify it thoroughly using the standards below before proceeding.
**4.** If payment is confirmed valid, use **Kimmy's Guard** to submit the transaction.
**5.** Wait for the confirmation embed to appear, then guide the customer through using the script until they're confident and self-sufficient.
**6.** When everything is in order and the customer has no further concerns, close the ticket.

### Payment Verification Standards

> ⚠ To prevent fraudulent payments: before submitting any payment or approving a whitelist, confirm that the proof of payment clearly shows **all** of the following.

- **Recipient name** matching the registered account — Sades Store / Nurlaela / Rian Febriansyah / [email protected]
- **Destination account number or QRIS**
- **Date and time of transfer** — if more than 5 minutes have passed between the transfer and when the screenshot was sent, treat it with suspicion
- **QRIS location** — Kab. Bandung / Bandung, 40376, ID
- **Transfer amount** — this is the most commonly falsified detail; verify it with extra care
- **Transaction reference or ID code** (if available)

**Proof of payment format requirements:**
- Do not accept screenshots that have been edited — filtered, heavily cropped, contrast-adjusted, etc.
- The image must be clear and unobscured by stickers, drawings, or blurring.
- The transfer amount and destination must not be cut off or hidden.
- Check for suspicious file names — such as names associated with photo editing apps.
- Reject screenshots that are cropped too short or missing key details.

### Public Chat Conduct

Public chat activity across all server channels counts toward your overall activity as a staff member. How you show up there matters just as much as how you handle tickets.

**1 · General Standards**
Maintain professional language in all public channels at all times. You represent Atomic in every message you send.
Stay composed and constructive — avoid conversations that could undermine your credibility as a staff member.
Do not engage in arguments or heated exchanges in public. If tension arises, de-escalate or redirect to a private channel.
You're welcome to be friendly and personable — but always carry yourself in a way that reflects well on the team.

**2 · Staff–Member Interaction Boundaries**
Keep a healthy professional distance from members you've only known through the server. Avoid becoming overly familiar in public channels.
If a member reaches out via DM and you don't know them well, maintain the same standards you'd apply in a public channel — helpful, clear, and professional.
If you've known a member for a long time and a casual dynamic exists between you, that's fine — but in public channels, professional tone still applies regardless of how well you know someone.
Avoid entering emotionally charged or personal exchanges with members — publicly or via DM — that could compromise your objectivity as staff.

**3 · Activity & Presence**
Public chat activity is counted toward your activity assessment. Being meaningfully present in the community — not just answering tickets — is part of your role as staff. Show up, engage, and make the server feel alive.
